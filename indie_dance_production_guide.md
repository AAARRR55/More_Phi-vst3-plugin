## 1. UNDERSTANDING INDIE DANCE & NEW HYPE INDIE DANCE

### Genre Definition

**Indie Dance** (also known as Indietronica) is a hybrid genre that emerged in the 1990s and 2000s, combining elements of:
- **Indie Rock**: Live instrumentation, songwriting focus, emotional depth
- **Electronic Production**: Synthesizers, drum machines, electronic processing
- **Post-Punk/New Wave**: Darker tones, raw energy, atmospheric textures

### Indie Dance vs Nu Disco

| Aspect | Indie Dance | Nu Disco |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Emotional Core** | Darker, raw, introspective | Joyful, celebratory, 70s-inspired |
| **Bass** | Gritty synth basslines, chorus/phaser | Funky, filtered bass |
| **Tempo** | 118-128 BPM | 115-125 BPM |
| **Key** | Often minor keys | Often major keys |
| **Instrumentation** | Live bass, guitars, synths | Purely electronic, vintage synths |
| **Vibe** | Warehouse/dark club | Poolside/daytime festival |

### New Hype Indie Dance (Modern Evolution)

The modern "New Hype Indie Dance" sound incorporates:
- **Tech House influences**: Tighter grooves, underground grit
- **Melodic Techno elements**: Atmospheric depth, evolving textures
- **Faster tempos**: Pushing 126-128 BPM
- **Hybrid production**: More electronic with organic touches
- **Labels**: Correspondant, Disco Halal, Roam Recordings, Radikon
- **Artists**: Peggy Gou, Curses, Moscoman, Terr, Lauer, Maceo Plex

## 2. PROJECT SETUP & FUNDAMENTALS

### Tempo & Key

| Parameter | Setting | Notes |

| **BPM** | 118-128 BPM | Sweet spot: 120-124 BPM (New Hype: 126-128) |
| **Key** | Minor keys preferred | A minor, D minor, F minor, G minor |

### Groove & Swing

Indie dance relies heavily on **groove and human feel**:
- Apply **8-12% swing** to hi-hats and percussion
- Use **track delay** (-5 to -15ms) on layered claps for organic feel
- Velocity variation: 60-95 for hi-hats, natural variation on all elements

## 3. DRUM PRODUCTION - ORGANIC MEETS ELECTRONIC

### The Indie Dance Drum Philosophy

Indie dance combines **acoustic drum textures** with **electronic precision**:
- Layer acoustic samples with synthesized hits
- Use vintage drum machine sounds (808, 909)
- Incorporate live drum loops and breakbeats
- Heavy use of percussion (shakers, tambourines, bongos)

### Kick Drum

**Layering Strategy:**
| Layer | Frequency | Purpose |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Sub Layer | 40-60 Hz | Low-end weight |
| Body Layer | 80-150 Hz | Punch and thump |
| Click Layer | 2-4 kHz | Attack definition |

**Processing Chain:**
```
EQ (HP @ 30Hz, cut @ 300Hz boxiness) → Compression (4:1) → 
Saturation (subtle analog warmth) → Sidechain trigger
```

### Snare & Clap

**Indie Dance Clap Technique:**
- Layer 2-3 different clap samples
- Use **track delay** (-10 to -20ms) on secondary layer
- Add reverb with pre-delay for space

**Snare Characteristics:**
- Often acoustic snares with body
- Ghost snares for groove (velocity 40-60)
- Rim shots for variation

### Hi-Hats & Percussion

**Essential Elements:**
- **Closed hats**: 1/16 notes with swing
- **Open hats**: Off-beat emphasis
- **Shakers**: Continuous 1/16 or 1/8 patterns
- **Tambourines**: On beats 2 and 4
- **Chimes/Congas**: For texture

**Processing:**
```
High-pass @ 300-500Hz → Stereo widening (subtle) → 
Reverb send (short plate) → Sidechain compression
```

### Drum Bus Processing

```
1. Glue Compression (2:1, slow attack, auto release)
2. Saturation (tape emulation, 10-15%)
3. Parallel Compression (aggressive, blended low)
4. Reverb (room or hall, 20% wet)
5. Light limiting
```

## 4. THE SIGNATURE INDIE DANCE BASS

### The Indie Dance Bass Sound

The **defining characteristic** of indie dance bass is the use of:
- **Chorus** (15-25% depth)
- **Phaser/Flanger** (subtle modulation)
- **Delay** (dotted 1/8 or 1/4 notes)
- **Saturation** for warmth

### Bass Sound Design

**Synthesis Approach:**
```
Oscillator: Saw or Square wave
- 2-3 unison voices
- Detune: 0.08-0.12 semitones

Filter: Low-pass, 24dB slope
- Cutoff: 200-600Hz (modulated)
- Resonance: 20-30%

Envelope:
- Attack: 5-20ms
- Decay: 200-400ms
- Sustain: 40-70%
- Release: 200-500ms
```

**Essential Modulation Chain:**
```
1. Chorus (Depth: 20%, Rate: 0.5-1Hz, Voices: 3)
2. Phaser (Rate: 1/4 or 1/2 note, Feedback: 25%, Mix: 30%)
3. Delay (Dotted 1/8, Feedback: 20-30%, High-pass @ 200Hz)
4. Saturation (Tube/Tape, Drive: 15-25%)
```

### Bass EQ Settings

| Frequency | Action | Amount | Purpose |
|-----------|--------|--------|---------|
| 30-40 Hz | High-pass | - | Clean sub |
| 60-100 Hz | Boost | +2-3 dB | Weight |
| 200-250 Hz | Cut | -2-3 dB | Prevent muddiness |
| 400-500 Hz | Cut | -1-2 dB | Remove boxiness |
| 1-2 kHz | Boost | +1-2 dB | Presence for small speakers |
| 3-5 kHz | Boost | +2-4 dB | Click/attack (optional) |

### Bass Processing Chain

```
1. EQ (surgical cuts)
2. Chorus (15-25% depth)
3. Phaser (subtle, rhythmic)
4. Delay (dotted, filtered)
5. Compression (4:1, medium attack/release)
6. Saturation (warmth and harmonics)
7. Sidechain compression (from kick)
8. Mono maker (below 120Hz)
```

### Bass Pattern Guidelines

- **Root-focused**: Most notes on root of chord
- **Octave jumps**: For variation and energy
- **Dotted rhythms**: Create groove and movement
- **Length**: Mix of short stabs and longer sustained notes

## 5. SYNTH DESIGN & MELODIC ELEMENTS

### Lead Synths

**Indie Dance Lead Characteristics:**
- Analog-modeled warmth (Diva, Prophet emulations)
- Detuned saw or pulse waves
- Portamento/glide for expressive bends
- Heavy use of LFO modulation

**Lead Sound Design:**
```
Oscillator A: Saw wave, 3-4 unison, detune 0.10
Oscillator B: Pulse wave, octave up, mix 30%

Filter: Low-pass, 12-18dB slope
- Cutoff: 2-8kHz (modulated)
- LFO to cutoff: Sine, 1/4 note rate, 20% depth

Envelope:
- Attack: 10-30ms (softened)
- Decay: 100-300ms
- Sustain: 70-85%
- Release: 300-800ms

Add: Portamento 50-150ms for glide between notes
```

### Plucks & Stabs

**Indie Dance Pluck Recipe:**
```
Oscillator: Saw or Square
- Short attack (1-5ms)
- Medium decay (100-200ms)
- Low sustain (20-40%)

Filter: Low-pass with envelope
- Cutoff starts high, closes quickly
- Envelope: Fast attack, medium decay

Effects:
- Chorus (15% depth)
- Delay (1/8 note, 25% feedback)
- Reverb (plate, 20% mix)
```

### Pads & Atmospheres

**Pad Design:**
```
Oscillator: Detuned saw or complex wavetable
- Slow attack (200-500ms)
- Long release (1-3 seconds)
- Multiple unison voices

LFO Modulation:
- Filter cutoff: Slow sine (0.1-0.3Hz)
- Wavetable position: 1/2 note rate
- Pan: Sine, slightly different rate

Processing:
- Chorus (25-35% depth)
- Reverb (long decay, 3-5 seconds)
- Delay (1/4 note dotted)
```

### Arpeggios

- **Rate**: 1/16 notes (with swing)
- **Waveform**: Triangle or saw
- **Octave range**: 2-3 octaves
- **Gate**: 70-85% for rhythmic tightness

## 6. VOCALS & HOOKS

### Vocal Style

Indie dance vocals range from:
- **Ethereal whispers**: Dreamy, reverb-soaked
- **Cool-toned monologues**: Spoken word, detached
- **Anthemic hooks**: Big, sing-along choruses
- **Chopped vocals**: Processed, rhythmic hooks

### Vocal Production Chain

```
1. Tuning (Melodyne, light correction)
2. Compression (2-3 stages: gentle then aggressive)
3. EQ (high-pass @ 80-100Hz, boost 3-5kHz presence)
4. De-esser (control sibilance)
5. Saturation (subtle warmth)
6. Reverb (long tail, 30-40% wet)
7. Delay (1/8 dotted, filtered)
8. Sidechain (to kick for pumping effect)
```

### Vocal Chops

**Technique:**
1. Sample vocal phrase
2. Chop into slices (1/8 or 1/16 notes)
3. Rearrange into new melody
4. Process with:
   - Pitch shifting (formant preservation)
   - Heavy compression
   - Stutter effects
   - Reverb/delay

### Creating Vocal Hooks

- **Repetition**: Simple, memorable phrases
- **Call and response**: Vocal question, synth answer
- **Layering**: Multiple takes for width
- **Processing**: Creative effects (reverse, granular)

## 8. MIXING CHAIN PROCESSING

### Standard Signal Chains

**KICK:**
```
EQ → Compression → Saturation (gentle) → Limiter
```

**BASS:**
```
EQ → Chorus → Phaser → Delay → Compression → 
Saturation → Sidechain (from kick) → Mono maker
```

**LEADS:**
```
EQ → Compression → Chorus → Delay → Reverb → 
Sidechain (subtle)
```

**PADS:**
```
EQ (HP @ 150Hz) → Chorus (20-30%) → Compression (2:1) → 
Reverb (long) → Delay → Sidechain
```

**VOCALS:**
```
Tuning → Compression → EQ → De-esser → Saturation → 
Reverb → Delay → Sidechain
```

### Drum Bus Chain

```
1. EQ (gentle high shelf +2dB, low cut @ 30Hz)
2. Glue Compressor (2:1, slow attack, auto release)
3. Saturation (tape, 10-15%)
4. Parallel Compression (8:1, blended low)
5. Stereo Enhancement (subtle above 8kHz)
6. Limiter (gentle, 1-2dB reduction)
```

### Master Bus Chain

```
1. Pro-Q 3 (low cut < 25Hz, gentle high shelf)
2. Saturn 2 (subtle warmth, Mix ~15-20%)
3. Bus Compressor (2:1, slow attack, auto release)
4. Stereo Enhancement (width above 3kHz)
5. Limiter (Pro-L 2, ceiling -0.8dB)
6. Meter (target -12 to -10 LUFS)
```

## 10. REVERB, DELAY & MODULATION

### Reverb Settings

**Main Reverb (Valhalla VintageVerb):**
```
Mode: 1970s Hall or Plate
Decay: 2.5-4 seconds
Pre-delay: 40-60ms
High-cut: 7-8kHz
Mix: 20-30%
```

**Vocal Reverb:**
```
Mode: Hall
Decay: 3-5 seconds
Pre-delay: 60-80ms
High-cut: 6kHz
Mix: 30-40%
```

**Drum Room:**
```
Mode: Room or Small Hall
Decay: 1-2 seconds
Pre-delay: 20ms
High-cut: 10kHz
Mix: 15-25%
```

### Delay Settings

**Dotted 1/8 Delay (Essential for Indie Dance):**
```
Time: 250-375ms (depending on BPM)
Feedback: 20-30%
High-pass: 200Hz
Low-pass: 5-6kHz
Mix: 20-25%
```

**1/4 Note Delay:**
```
Time: 500-600ms
Feedback: 25-35%
High-pass: 300Hz
Mix: 15-20%
```

**Slapback Delay (for guitars/vocals):**
```
Time: 60-120ms
Feedback: 10-15%
Mix: 20-30%
```

### Modulation Settings

**Chorus (for Bass):**
```
Rate: 0.5-1 Hz
Depth: 15-25%
Voices: 3
Mix: 30-50%
```

**Phaser (for Bass/Movement):**
```
Rate: 1/4 or 1/2 note (synced)
Feedback: 20-30%
Mix: 25-40%
Stages: 4-6
```

**Flanger (for Leads):**
```
Rate: Slow (0.1-0.3 Hz) or synced
Feedback: 30-50%
Mix: 20-30%
```

## 11. ARRANGEMENT & STRUCTURE

### Standard Indie Dance Structure

| Section | Length | Description |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Intro** | 16-32 bars | Drums, bass, atmosphere building |
| **Verse 1** | 16 bars | Add melodic elements, reduce drums |
| **Pre-Chorus** | 8 bars | Build tension, filter automation |
| **Chorus/Drop** | 16 bars | Full energy, all elements |
| **Verse 2** | 16 bars | Variation, remove some elements |
| **Pre-Chorus** | 8 bars | Build again |
| **Chorus/Drop** | 16 bars | Full energy |
| **Breakdown** | 16-32 bars | Strip to pads, vocals, atmosphere |
| **Build-Up** | 8-16 bars | Rising tension, risers |
| **Final Drop** | 16-32 bars | Maximum energy |
| **Outro** | 16-32 bars | Strip elements, DJ-friendly |

### Energy Management

- **Start lower energy**: Build throughout track
- **Breakdown contrast**: Strip drums, focus on melody/vocals
- **Filter automation**: Gradual opening during builds
- **Element variation**: Add/remove every 8 bars

### DJ-Friendly Considerations

- **Extended intro**: 16-32 bars of drums/bass
- **Extended outro**: Similar to intro for mixing
- **Consistent energy**: Don't drop suddenly
- **Clear phrases**: 8-bar sections with defined changes

## 13. LCD SOUNDSYSTEM & DFA PRODUCTION DEEP DIVE

### The DFA Records Sound Philosophy

James Murphy and DFA Records established the blueprint for modern indie dance. Their production philosophy centers on **analog imperfection meets electronic precision**:

**Core Principles:**
- "Good idea beats talent" - Focus on songwriting over technical perfection
- Use of vintage gear with deliberate limitations
- Live jamming approach - tracks built from performances, not programming
- Embrace of "mistakes" that add character
- Cowbells, handclaps, and organic percussion

### James Murphy's Gear & Techniques

**Favorite Drum Machines:**
| Machine | Use Case | Characteristics |
|---------|----------|-----------------|
| **Roland TR-808** | Kick foundation | Deep sub, iconic punch |
| **Roland TR-606** | Snare/hi-hats | Tighter, more aggressive |
| **Wurlitzer Sideman** | Sub-layer kick | "Makes you feel like bottom dropped out" |

**Essential Synths (from James Murphy's Studio):**
```
1. EMS Synthi A - "Magical beast" - Used for "Someone Great" chords
   - Joystick-controlled pitch fluxing
   - Filter everything through it
   - Capable of animal noises, chords, and chaos

2. Roland Jupiter 4 - "Soft and lush"
   - Distinct gentle harmoniousness
   - Pad sounds and atmospheric elements
   - Recently MIDI-modded for better control

3. Roland System 100M - "Bassline killer"
   - Modular system with SH-101 integration
   - Fuzzy, driven bass sounds
   - Used live with multiple units

4. Roland SH-101 - Live performance
   - CV/Gate/clock sync capabilities
   - Bass and lead duties
   - Murphy had 3 per live rig

5. Yamaha CS-60 - DFA Mix Staple
   - Featured on 'Get Innocuous'
   - Wonky, liquid beat creation
   - Drum sounds created from synth hits

6. Korg microKORG - Live secret weapon
   - Copies vintage studio sounds for stage
   - "Can get nearly any sound out of it"
   - Never used for sound generation, only replication
```

**Microphone Chain (DFA Studio Standard):**
```

DBX 165 Compressor → LA-2A Leveling Amplifier

For darker, vintage vocal sound:
- Ribbon mics: Beyerdynamic M160/M201, Coles 4033

- Characteristic warmth and midrange punch
- Simple, consistent signal path

**Drum Recording (1957 Gretsch Jazz Kit):**
```
- "No way to tune or mic them to make them sound bad"
- Natural room sound emphasized

## 15. ADVANCED DRUM PROGRAMMING

### The Dance-Punk Groove (LCD/DFA Style)

**Beat Pattern (16 steps):**
```
KICK:  X...X...X...X... (1, 5, 9, 13 - but variations common)
       Add ghost kicks: ..X.....X.....X. (velocity 40-60)

SNARE: ....X.......X... (5, 13 - backbeat emphasis)
       Add ghost snares: velocity 30-50 on off-beats

HI-HAT: X.X.X.X.X.X.X.X. (off-beat emphasis)
        Two different samples alternating
        
OPEN HAT: .....X.......X.. (accent on 6, 14)
```

**Humanization Techniques:**
```yaml
TIMING VARIATIONS:
  Hi-hats: ±5-15ms (nudge right for "laid back" feel)
  Ghost snares: ±10-20ms
  Shakers: ±20-30ms (more is better for organic feel)
  
VELOCITY RANGES:
  Main kick: 110-127
  Ghost kicks: 40-70
  Main snare: 100-120
  Ghost snares: 25-50
  Hi-hats: 60-95 (accents on 120+)
  Shakers: 40-80 continuous variation
```

### Creating the "Live Jam" Feel

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Program basic pattern on grid
2. Apply 8-12% swing (shuffle)
3. Manually adjust velocities for human feel
4. Add micro-timing variations (±10ms)
5. Layer 2-3 hi-hat samples with different timings
6. Add shakers slightly off-grid
7. Apply groove template from classic breaks

**Groove Templates to Study:**
- Clyde Stubblefield (James Brown breaks)
- D'Angelo neo-soul grooves
- Live disco drummers (Bernard Purdie)

### Acoustic + Electronic Layering Matrix

| Element | Electronic Layer | Acoustic Layer | Blend |
|---------|------------------|----------------|-------|
| Kick | 808/909 sub | Acoustic kick body | 60/40 |
| Snare | 707/MPC sample | Real snare crack | 50/50 |
| Hats | 808 closed | Real hi-hat shimmer | 40/60 |
| Clap | 909 clap | Real handclaps | 30/70 |
| Perc | Synth percussion | Shakers/Tambourine | 20/80 |

## 16. MID/SIDE PROCESSING FOR INDIE DANCE

### Mid/Side EQ Strategy

**Bass Management:**
```yaml
LOW FREQUENCY (M/S):
  SIDE CHANNEL:
    High-pass @ 120Hz (24dB/octave)
    Purpose: Keep sub frequencies mono
  
  MID CHANNEL:
    Boost +2dB @ 80Hz (Q=1.0)
    Purpose: Focus bass power in center
```

**Creating Width in Synths:**
```yaml
SYNTH LEADS M/S EQ:
  SIDE CHANNEL:
    Boost +3dB shelf @ 8kHz
    Boost +2dB @ 4kHz (Q=0.8)
    Purpose: High-frequency width
  
  MID CHANNEL:
    Cut -1dB @ 4kHz
    Boost +2dB @ 1.5kHz
    Purpose: Presence without harshness
```

**Vocal Placement:**
```yaml
VOCAL M/S:
  MID CHANNEL:
    Boost +2dB @ 3kHz (presence)
    Cut -2dB @ 400Hz (mud removal)
  
  SIDE CHANNEL:
    High-pass @ 400Hz
    Cut -3dB @ 2-5kHz
    Purpose: Keep vocal centered, reverb wide
```

**Dynamic Width Control:**
```yaml
PAD BUS COMPRESSION:
  MID CHANNEL:
    Ratio: 4:1
    Attack: 30ms
    Release: 200ms

  SIDE CHANNEL:
    Ratio: 2:1
    Attack: 10ms
    Release: 100ms
    Threshold: -12dB
    Effect: Maintains side information during peaks
```

## 18. ADVANCED VOCAL TECHNIQUES

### Indie Dance Vocal Production Chain

**Recording Stage:**
```yaml
CHAIN:
  Sennheiser MD 409 → UA Preamp → 
  DBX 165 → LA-2A (if available)
  
ALTERNATIVE BUDGET CHAIN:
  Shure SM7B → Cloudlifter → 
  Warm Audio WA-73 → 1176-style comp
```

**Processing Chain (Detailed):**
```yaml
1. TUNING (Melodyne):
   - Correction speed: 30-50%
   - Preserve natural vibrato
   - Formant correction: OFF (unless extreme)

2. FIRST COMPRESSION (Gentle):
   Ratio: 2:1
   Attack: 10ms
   Release: Auto
   Gain reduction: 2-4dB

3. SECOND COMPRESSION (Character):
   Ratio: 4:1
   Attack: 5ms
   Release: 100ms
   Gain reduction: 4-6dB

4. EQ:
   High-pass: 80Hz (12dB/oct)
   Cut: -2dB @ 250Hz (mud)
   Cut: -1dB @ 800Hz (boxiness)
   Boost: +3dB @ 3.5kHz (presence)
   Boost: +2dB shelf @ 12kHz (air)

5. DE-ESSER:
   Frequency: 6-8kHz
   Threshold: -20dB
   Reduction: 3-6dB max

6. SATURATION:
   Type: Tape emulation
   Drive: 10-15%
   Mix: 25%

7. SPATIAL:
   Reverb: Hall, 3s decay, 30% wet
   Delay: 1/8 dotted, 20% mix, filtered
```

### Creating Vocal Hooks

**Chop Technique (Step-by-Step):**
```yaml
STEP 1: SOURCE
  Record or sample vocal phrase
  Length: 2-4 bars
  
STEP 2: SLICING
  Slice at transient points
  Grid: 1/16 or 1/32 notes
  
STEP 3: REARRANGE
  Create new melody from slices
  Use pitch shifts (±5 semitones)
  Maintain formants for realism
  
STEP 4: PROCESSING
  - Heavy compression (8:1 ratio)
  - Saturation for grit
  - Short reverb (room, 20%)
  - Sidechain to kick
```

## 19. TROUBLESHOOTING COMMON ISSUES

### Problem: Bass Lacks Indie Dance Character

**Diagnosis Checklist:**
- [ ] Chorus applied? (15-25% depth)
- [ ] Phaser on synced rate? (1/4 or 1/2 note)
- [ ] Dotted delay present? (filtered)
- [ ] Saturation added? (15-25%)
- [ ] Sidechain compression engaged?

**Solution Chain:**
```
Bass → EQ (cuts) → Chorus (20%) → Phaser (synced 1/4) → 
Delay (dotted 1/8, filtered) → Compression (4:1) → 
Saturation (20%) → Sidechain (kick) → Mono (<120Hz)
```

### Problem: Drums Sound Too Rigid

**Humanization Steps:**
1. Apply 10% swing to hi-hats
2. Manually vary velocities (60-95 range)
3. Nudge shakers +15ms off grid
4. Layer 2 different clap samples with -15ms offset
5. Add ghost snares at velocity 40
6. Use groove template from soul/disco breaks

### Problem: Mix Sounds Thin

**Indie Dance Warmth Recipe:**
```yaml
MASTER BUS:
  Saturn 2: 
    - Tube saturation, 15% mix
    - Focus on 200-800Hz range
  
  Parallel Compression:
    - Duplicate drum bus
    - Heavy compression (10:1)
    - Blend at -15dB
  
  Tape Emulation:
    - J37 or similar
    - Input: +3dB (soft saturation)
    - Output: -3dB
```
